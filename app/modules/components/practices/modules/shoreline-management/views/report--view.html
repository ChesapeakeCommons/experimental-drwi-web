Adaptive Management Checks Performed
  Monitored on
{{ report.properties.report_date }}
Hydrology/Geomorphology
Has the designed hydrologic regime established itself? If not, revisit design to improve inflow/outflow.
Do built features and natural structural elements continue to meet design specifications?
Is the wetland elevation stable or increasing or positive sediment accretion noted? If not, several options should be considered including adding/redesigning/re-locating structures (groins, sand fences) to promote sediment deposition or increasing vegetation in the marsh.
Are the various interim wetlands metric values continuing to approach those at reference sites?
Biota
Has planned vegetation established itself, as expected? If growth of initial plant species is sub-optimal due to excessive wave energy, consider other taxa with more appropriate growth requirements.
Have invasive species become prevelant? If so consider herbicide use or periodic manual removal.
Have muskrats, nutria, or other mammals altered planned development of the wetland? If so, consider fencing, trapping, or hunting to manage populations.
Is fauna ‘success’ as high as anticipated? If not, evaluate whether fish use limitations might indicate low connectivity, and whether low numbers of young birds or mammals might suggest high predation.
Physico-chemical
Do pore or surface salinities or grain size and %OM match reference wetlands values? If not, examine connectivity to surrounding waters.
Do pH values match reference wetlands values? If not, adjust pH with lime.
Do denitrification rates and plant growth match reference wetlands values? If not, increase OM.
